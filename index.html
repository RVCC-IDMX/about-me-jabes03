Background image properties: repeat, size, position, origin, and clip.

Background images can attract the reader’s eyes to the content you want them to see, but they also can be obstructing the content you want them to see.

Background images can attract the reader’s eyes to the content you want them to see, but they also can be obstructing the content you want them to see.

Background images come with many properties so that you can attract your viewer’s eyes in different and creative ways.

The picture of a field that is behind this text is an example of how this image can be used.

But background images don’t already come perfectly placed for your text. According to W3 Schools (a website about online learning) “By default, a background-image is placed at the top-left corner of an element, and repeated both vertically and horizontally. . .The background of an element is the total size of the element, including padding and border (but not the margin) . . . Always set a background-color to be used if the image is unavailable.” (W3Schools CSS background-image property). So there is some kind of tinkering you will need to do after you paste your background image.

So if you want to add a background image to your text or the whole website, a simple

body {

background-image:

}

will not suffice. You will need to make use of the properties that come with the background images. If not, your background image will be a repeated image going downwards with the maximum opacity, making the text harder to read. As said in this website that helps you understand the coding rules “The background-repeat CSS property sets how background images are repeated. A background image can be repeated along the horizontal and vertical axes, or not repeated at all.” (MozDevNet).

Some of the main properties background images have are the following:

  • Repeat: “Sets if/how a background image will be repeated.”(W3Schools CSS)
  • Size: “Sets the size of the element's background image.” (MozDevNet)
  • Position: “Sets the initial position for each background image.” (MozDevNet)
  • Origin: “Specifies the origin position (the background positioning area) of a background image.” (W3Schools CSS)
  • Clip: “Sets whether an element's background extends underneath its border box, padding box, or content box.”(MozDevNet)

As you can see, there is a big variety of things to do with the background image, and each property can achieve different goals.

But, background images do not only mean adding a graphic photo behind your text. You can also add plain colors so that the text can stand out. One famous example is in code.
